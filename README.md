### Initial ideas
## Project Topic
This project explores climate-related risks affecting U.S. National Parks.

## Project Description
We will create an interactive web map that shows the locations of U.S. National Parks and highlights different climate risks that may affect them, such as wildfire, drought, flooding, sea level rise, and extreme heat.

## Project Goal
The goal of this project is to help users understand how climate change affects national parks across different regions of the United States.

## Target Users
- Students
- Researchers
- National park visitors
- Members of the general public interested in climate change

## Use Case Scenario
A user opens the map and selects a national park to learn what climate risks may affect that park. The user can compare parks in different parts of the country and explore how climate risks vary by region.

### Functional Requirements
## Representation Requirements
- Display U.S. National Parks on an interactive map
- Show park locations with markers or symbols
- Represent different climate risks using colors, categories, or layers
- Include popups with park information and climate risk details

## Interaction Requirements
- Zoom and pan around the map
- Click on a park to view more information
- Search for a park by name
- Turn climate risk layers on and off
- Use a legend to understand symbols and colors

### Data Sources
- National Park Service park location data
- Climate risk datasets related to wildfire, drought, flooding, sea level rise, or heat
